"Let's Talk": Digital Prototype

Background

In my class, Methods for Human-Centered Computing, I worked with a group to create a solution for a problem space of our choosing, our problem space being mental health. We centered our project around the problem statement that IU students struggle to gain financial access to personalized and appropriate mental health resources. From our research, we created a digital prototype of a mental health application called “Let’s Talk.”

Our Process

Choosing a Problem Space

We started our project off by choosing a problem space: Mental Health. We worked as a group to figure out what kinds of problems there are within this problem space and then took time to think through the users that would be affected by these problems. After discussing all of it, we discovered our problem statement: IU students lack the financial resources to gain mental health resources.

Conducting Primary and Secondary Research

Once we decided on our problem statement, we began our own research through finding acedemic articles that supported what we believed to be the problem within the space of mental health. Afterwards, we created survey and interview questions that would then be used to gain data about our problem space.

Creating an Affinity Diagram

After conducting primary and secondary research, we moved on to create an affinty diagram where we out all of our ideas, ideations, and sketches.

Creating User Personas

We eventually created user personas to determine who would be using our application should we decide to go with the idea.

Creating Paper Prototypes

We all created paper prototypes of our ideas and then decided on a single idea to continue with, while also adding some of the elements from other ideas that we liked.

Creating Digital Prototype

We finally decided on our final design and created a fully functional digital prototype of our mental health application. This application is called "Let's Talk" and is used as an application for free mental health resources. There are meditations that the users can listen to that are personalized to their personal needs. There is a recording option for voice memos where there is a sentiment analysis machine-learning system that will pick out key words from the session. There are also options for getting into contact with a therapist, and the application shows information about their values, approaches to therapy, and the user can contact them if they're open to new clients.
